Outfit Formulas
Business Casual — Daily Wear
The Sharp Casual
  • Top: Fitted crew-neck sweater in navy, forest green, or camel
  • Pants: Slim-fit chinos in charcoal, dark olive, or black
  • Shoes: White leather low-top sneakers or clean suede loafers
  • Layer: Unstructured blazer in camel or charcoal (optional)
  • Accessory: Simple chain necklace + minimalist watch
Social Events — Dinner, Outings
The Intentional Look
  • Top: Short-sleeve camp collar shirt (white, ecru, or deep blue) — you already nail this
  • Pants: Slim straight black trousers or dark indigo denim
  • Shoes: White leather sneakers or black Chelsea boots
  • Accessory: Chain necklace + dark sunglasses + watch
  • Tip: Leave 1–2 buttons open, shirt untucked but fitted
Smart Casual — Weekends
The Layered Classic
  • Base: Plain white or cream fitted tee
  • Layer: Overshirt in deep blue denim, forest green, or burnt sienna
  • Pants: Slim chinos in camel or slate gray — NOT black joggers
  • Shoes: Clean white sneakers or tan suede trainers
  • Tip: Leave overshirt unbuttoned — creates vertical line that elongates
Evening / Going Out
The Statement
  • Top: Rich-colored fitted turtleneck or mock-neck (wine, forest green, camel)
  • Pants: Black slim trousers or dark denim
  • Shoes: Black leather Chelsea boots or clean white sneakers
  • Jacket: Leather bomber or structured wool coat in camel or black
The 5 Rules For Your Body
1
Always go slim-fit, never baggy
Your lean build disappears in oversized clothes. Slim-fit shows your frame without being tight. Shirts should skim the torso, not hang.
2
Upgrade your bottoms — they're your weak spot
Your photos show great tops paired with casual bottoms (joggers, basic shorts). Slim chinos or dark straight denim instantly elevates any look.
3
Use vertical lines to add perceived height
Open overshirts, V-necks, and camp collars all create a vertical line that makes you look taller. Avoid wide horizontal stripes.
4
Your shoes should match the outfit's energy
White sneakers are versatile but chunky trainers with trousers kills the look. Black Chelsea boots are the highest-value upgrade you can make.
5
Lean into rich, saturated color — you can handle it
Most men with your skin tone play it too safe with grey and black. Deep navy, forest green, wine, camel, and burnt orange look exceptional on you.
